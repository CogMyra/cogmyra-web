CogMyra Guide – Privacy & Data Protection

Effective Date: August 11, 2025 · Last Updated: August 11, 2025

This page explains how CogMyra Guide (“CogMyra”, “CogMyra Guide”, “CMG”, “we”, “us”, or “our”) collects, uses, and protects information about learners, parents and guardians, educators, and professionals. It is designed to be easy to read and accessible for families, schools, and partners. The full legal text of our Privacy Policy is included below.

If you have any questions at any time, you can contact us at info@cogmyra.com.

Quick Overview (Accordion)

Use the sections below to see a short explanation of our privacy and data practices. For the full legal Privacy Policy, scroll down or jump directly to Full Privacy Policy.

CogMyra Guide is a personalized AI learning platform designed for learners of all ages, including minors, as well as parents, educators, and professionals. This Privacy Policy applies whenever you use CogMyra Guide, whether on your own, through a parent or guardian, or as part of a school or institution.

We follow relevant privacy and education laws, including COPPA (for children’s online privacy), FERPA (for educational records in schools that use CogMyra), and GDPR (for users in the European Union and other applicable regions).

We collect a limited set of information so we can personalize learning, keep the platform secure, and meet our legal obligations. This includes:

  • Profile information such as name or display name, email address, age or date of birth (for age gating and parental consent), role (student, parent, educator, etc.), and educational level.
  • Learning interactions and artifacts such as the questions you ask, the responses you receive from the AI, and any saved summaries, plans, or learning outputs.
  • Emotional and engagement signals that you choose to share in your messages (for example, saying you feel “stressed,” “confused,” or “excited”) to help the system adjust its tone and pacing.
  • Technical data like browser type, device type, IP address, approximate region, and error logs so we can keep the platform secure and functioning properly.

We do not sell personal information. We use data only to provide and improve the learning experience, support schools, and meet our legal and security requirements.

CogMyra Guide is designed to work safely with children and students:

  • For children under the applicable age threshold (typically under 13 in the United States), we use age gating and obtain verifiable parental or guardian consent before collecting personal information beyond what is strictly necessary for basic functionality.
  • When CogMyra is provided through a school or district, some information may be considered educational records under FERPA. In those cases, we act as a service provider to the school, and the school remains the primary data controller for education records.
  • We limit data collection from minors to what is needed for educational purposes, and parents/guardians can request access, correction, or deletion of their child’s information.

Parents, guardians, and schools can contact us any time at info@cogmyra.com with questions about children’s data.

Depending on where you live and how you use CogMyra Guide, you may have rights such as:

  • Requesting a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Requesting corrections to inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Requesting deletion of your personal data (“Right to be Forgotten”) when it is no longer needed or when processing is based on consent you withdraw.
  • Requesting your personal data in a portable, machine-readable format, and in some cases asking us to transfer it to another service.
  • Objecting to or requesting restriction of certain types of processing, particularly when based on legitimate interests.

Parents and guardians may exercise these rights on behalf of minors. To make a request, please email info@cogmyra.com and we will guide you through the process.

We keep data only as long as it is needed for learning, safety, and compliance, then we delete or de-identify it. In general:

  • Profile and account data (such as name, email, role): kept while the account is active and for a limited period (typically up to 3 years) afterward for legal, security, and reactivation purposes.
  • Learning progress and history: kept while the account is active and for a limited period (typically up to 3 years) to support long-term learning continuity and institutional needs.
  • Events, emotional signals, and artifacts: typically retained for around 180 days to support recent learning continuity and safety checks, then deleted or anonymized.
  • Technical logs and diagnostics: typically retained for around 12 months to support security monitoring and troubleshooting, then deleted or anonymized.

When you request deletion, we will remove or de-identify your personal data as described in the full policy and subject to our legal obligations.

We use a combination of technical, administrative, and organizational safeguards to protect your data. These include encryption, access controls, monitoring and logging, backup and recovery procedures, and incident response processes for handling potential security events.

While no system can be perfectly secure, we work continuously to reduce risk and strengthen our defenses. Schools, districts, and partners can request additional technical details by contacting info@cogmyra.com.

We work with carefully selected service providers (for hosting, infrastructure, and related services) under contracts that require strong privacy and security protections. These providers may process data on our behalf but are not allowed to use it for their own purposes.

We do not sell personal information. When data is transferred across borders, we apply safeguards consistent with GDPR and other applicable laws.

Full Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy describes how CogMyra (“CogMyra,” “CogMyra Guide,” “CMG,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, and protects information about users of the CogMyra Guide AI learning platform, including learners, parents and guardians, educators, and professionals.

We are committed to protecting your privacy and handling your data in a safe, transparent, and compliant way. We design CogMyra Guide to support personalized learning while respecting applicable laws, including:

  • Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)
  • Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) where used by schools
  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) where applicable

If you do not agree with this Policy, please do not use CogMyra Guide.

1. Information We Collect

We collect three broad categories of information:

1.1 Information You Provide Directly

This includes:

  • Account and profile details such as name or display name, email address, age or date of birth (to support age gating and parental consent), role (e.g., student, parent, educator, professional), and educational level.
  • Learning interactions and content such as questions you ask and inputs you provide to the AI, responses generated by the system in your sessions, and saved “artifacts” such as summaries, plans, and exported materials.
  • Support and communications such as messages you send to us for help, feedback you provide about the platform, and participation in surveys, pilots, or research studies.

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you use CogMyra Guide, we automatically collect certain technical and usage data, such as:

  • Browser and device type, operating system, and basic device settings.
  • IP address and approximate location (country or region level, not precise GPS).
  • Session activity, timestamps, and error logs.
  • An anonymous user identifier (for example, a cookie or similar identifier such as cmg_uid) used to remember your session, preferences, and learning history on a given device.

1.3 Emotional and Behavioral Signals

CogMyra Guide may infer or receive information about your emotional state or engagement, for example when you indicate you feel “stressed,” “confused,” or “excited,” or when similar signals appear in your messages.

We use these signals only to adjust tone, pacing, and support to improve the quality of the learning experience. We treat this type of information with particular care and do not use it for unrelated purposes such as advertising.

2. How We Use Your Information

2.1 To Provide and Improve CogMyra Guide

  • Personalize learning content, pacing, and explanations.
  • Track progress and help you resume where you left off.
  • Save and organize your artifacts, lessons, and outputs.
  • Maintain the security and integrity of the platform.
  • Debug, monitor, and improve performance and reliability.

2.2 To Support Compliance and Safety

  • Verify age and identify when parental consent is required.
  • Comply with school or institutional agreements, where applicable.
  • Monitor for misuse, abuse, or security threats.
  • Respond to legal requests or regulatory inquiries, if required by law.

2.3 To Communicate With You

  • Send important notices about your account or changes to this Policy.
  • Respond to support requests and feedback.
  • Share educational content or updates about CogMyra Guide, where permitted by your preferences and applicable law.

We do not sell your personal information.

3. Legal Bases for Processing (GDPR)

Where GDPR applies, we process personal data under one or more of the following legal bases:

  • Consent: When you (or your parent/guardian, for minors) give clear permission, especially for account creation, use of certain data types, or participation in pilots or research.
  • Performance of a contract: To provide the educational services you request.
  • Legal obligations: To meet obligations under applicable laws (for example, COPPA, FERPA, tax, and audit requirements).
  • Legitimate interests: To maintain and improve platform security and functionality, conduct internal analytics in de-identified form, and develop new features— balanced against your rights and expectations.

You may withdraw consent at any time where consent is the basis for processing.

4. Children’s Privacy and Parental Consent

CogMyra Guide is designed for learners of different ages, including minors. For users under the relevant age threshold (typically under 13 in the United States), we:

  • Use age gating to identify minors and trigger a parental or guardian consent process before collecting personal information beyond what is strictly necessary for basic functionality.
  • Limit data collection from children to what is necessary for educational purposes.
  • Provide parents and guardians with the right to review their child’s information, request corrections, or request deletion of the child’s personal data, subject to legal and operational requirements.

If we learn that we have collected personal information from a child without appropriate consent, we will delete it as soon as reasonably possible.

5. Educational Records (FERPA)

When CogMyra Guide is provided to students through a school, district, or educational institution:

  • Some information may be considered “education records” under FERPA.
  • We treat the school or institution as the primary data controller for those records and act as a service provider/processor.
  • We only use education records consistent with our agreement with the institution and applicable law.
  • We do not use FERPA-covered education records for targeted advertising.

Schools and institutions are responsible for providing required notices to students and families and for handling certain FERPA rights requests. We support them as specified in our agreements.

6. Data Sharing and Third Parties

We do not sell personal data.

We may share information in limited circumstances, including:

  • Service providers and vendors: Companies that help us operate the platform (such as hosting providers, infrastructure services, or email delivery tools). These providers may access data only to perform services on our behalf and are bound by contractual obligations to protect confidentiality and comply with applicable laws.
  • Schools and institutions: When CogMyra Guide is used through a school or institution, we may share relevant information with authorized school staff according to our agreement with the institution and applicable law.
  • Legal and safety reasons: When required by law, regulation, legal process, or government request; or to protect the rights, property, or safety of CogMyra, our users, or the public.

If CogMyra is involved in a merger, acquisition, or similar transaction, we may transfer information as part of that transaction, subject to continued protection consistent with this Policy.

7. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Policy or as required by law. In general:

  • Account and profile information: Retained while your account is active and for a reasonable period afterward (typically up to 3 years) for purposes such as reactivation, legal obligations, dispute resolution, and security.
  • Learning progress and interaction history: Retained while your account is active and for a reasonable period afterward (typically up to 3 years) to support learning continuity, institutional needs, and internal analytics in aggregate or de-identified form.
  • Events, emotional and behavioral signals, and certain artifacts: Typically retained for a shorter window (for example, around 180 days) to support recent learning continuity, personalization, and safety, then deleted or de-identified.
  • Technical logs and diagnostic data: Typically retained for about 12 months to support security monitoring, troubleshooting, and performance analysis, then deleted or de-identified.

When data is no longer needed, we either delete it securely or de-identify it so it can be used in aggregate, non-personal form (for example, to understand feature usage or improve the system) without identifying individuals.

If you request deletion of your data, we will honor the request consistent with our Data Deletion / Right to be Forgotten processes and subject to any legal requirements to retain certain records.

8. Cookies and Similar Technologies

CogMyra Guide uses cookies or similar technologies primarily to:

  • Remember you between sessions (for example, via an anonymous identifier like cmg_uid).
  • Maintain session security and integrity.
  • Understand basic usage patterns to improve the service.

Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies through settings. If you disable cookies, some features of CogMyra Guide may not function properly.

We do not use cookies to track you across unrelated third-party websites for advertising purposes.

9. Security Measures

We use a combination of technical, administrative, and organizational safeguards designed to protect your data, including:

  • Encryption of data in transit and at rest where appropriate.
  • Access controls and role-based permissions for staff and systems.
  • Regular security and compliance reviews.
  • Backup and recovery procedures to maintain availability and integrity.
  • Monitoring, logging, and incident response procedures to detect and manage potential security events.

No system can be guaranteed to be 100% secure, but we work continuously to reduce risk and improve our defenses.

10. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your location and how you use CogMyra Guide, you may have one or more of the following rights:

  • Access: Request a copy of your personal data that we hold.
  • Correction: Request that inaccurate or incomplete information be corrected.
  • Deletion (“Right to be Forgotten”): Request deletion of your personal data when it is no longer necessary for the purposes collected, or when processing is based on consent you have withdrawn, subject to legal exceptions.
  • Portability: Request your personal data in a commonly used, machine-readable format and, where feasible, request that we transfer it directly to another service.
  • Objection / Restriction: Object to or request restriction of certain processing activities, especially where processing is based on legitimate interests.
  • Consent Withdrawal: Withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on your consent (without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal).

For minors, parents or legal guardians may exercise these rights on the child’s behalf.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at info@cogmyra.com. We may need to verify your identity (and, for minors, your relationship to the child) before processing your request.

If you are not satisfied with how we handle your request, you may have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection authority or other regulator in your jurisdiction.

11. International Data Transfers

CogMyra may store and process data in countries other than the one where you live. When we transfer personal data across borders, we take steps to ensure appropriate safeguards are in place consistent with applicable laws (for example, standard contractual clauses where required).

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in the law, technology, or our services.

  • When we make material changes, we will provide a clear notice (for example, via the platform or by email) and indicate the “Last Updated” date at the top of the Policy.
  • Your continued use of CogMyra Guide after changes take effect means you accept the revised Policy.

We encourage you to review this page periodically.
